## FAQs

AI

## What are the key differences between supervised and unsupervised learning methods?

Supervised learning relies on labeled datasets for prediction, while unsupervised learning deals with unlabeled data to uncover patterns. In supervised learning, models are trained to predict outcomes, whereas unsupervised learning focuses on clustering and dimensionality reduction.

## How does ensemble learning improve machine learning model performance?

Ensemble learning combines multiple algorithms to enhance accuracy and robustness over individual models. Techniques like boosting and bagging address variance and overfitting issues, demonstrating significant performance improvements, especially in classification tasks.

## What is the role of K-means clustering in data analysis?

K-means clustering groups objects based on feature similarity, optimizing intra-cluster similarity while maximizing inter-cluster distance. This centroid-based approach effectively partitions data into K clusters, facilitating insights in large datasets.

## How do neural networks mimic human brain functionality?

Neural networks consist of interconnected nodes that simulate the biological neuron structure, enabling complex computations akin to human thought processes. They effectively model relationships in data through layers of interconnected neurons, imitating learning behaviors.

## What challenges arise from high-dimensional data in machine learning?

As dimensionality increases, the volume of space expands, leading to data sparsity that complicates statistical computations. Techniques like discriminant analysis and PCA are essential for reducing dimensionality and mitigating these challenges.
